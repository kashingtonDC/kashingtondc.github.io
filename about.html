Background

I was born in Totonto, Canada to parents from Bangalore, India and Nairobi, Kenya. I hold 2.5 citizenships (USA, Canada, Overseas Citizen of India). I grew up in Free Acres, Berkeley Heights, New Jersey, where my childhood was spent exploring the woods and causing mischeif around the neighborhood. I studied at Franklin and Marshall College under Dr. Dorothy Merritts and at Boston College under Dr. Noah P. Snyder. I currently work with Dr. John D. Bolten at NASA Goddard and am in search of PhD opportunities!

Hobbies and Interests

I am a life long learner who is always trying to pick up new skills and hone those already in the repetoire. I have a passion for applying knowledge to help others, especially in scenarios of disasters or humanitarian relief. I have loved public speaking ever since delivering a middle school commencement speech to 1000+ people as salutatorian. I lift weights and run on a regular basis, and bicycle and hike as often as possible. I enjoy playing tennis and basketball and used to play many other sports. I am a capable guitarist and passable pianist and drummer. I read voraciously and in thematic bursts. My recent kick has been Michael Lewis books on finance and investing. I'm slowly turning in to a decent cook and a morning person. I have traveled to 5 continents and intend to make it to them all.
